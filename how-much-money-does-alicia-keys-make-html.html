Alicia Augello Cook (born January 25, 1981), known professionally as Alicia Keys, is an American singer-songwriter. A classically-trained pianist, Keys was composing songs by age 12. She was signed at 15 years old by Columbia Records, and then, after label disputes over creative differences, by Arista Records. Her debut album, Songs in A Minor was released with J Records in 2001.

As I Am (2007) produced the Hot 100 number-one single "No One", selling 5 million copies worldwide and earning an additional three Grammy Awards. Keys made her film debut in Smokin' Aces and also went on to appear in The Nanny Diaries and The Secret Life of Bees. The Element of Freedom (2009) became her first chart-topping album in the UK, and selling 4 million copies worldwide.

Keys has received numerous awards throughout her career, including 15 competitive Grammy Awards, 17 NAACP Image Awards and 12 ASCAP Awards. She has sold over 35 million albums and 30 million singles worldwide.
