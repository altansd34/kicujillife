The Voice Season 21 star Sasha Allen recently made a huge announcement on TikTok, sharing that he has signed with Republic Records. The 20-year-old added that he actually signed two record deals, including one with his father Jim Allen.

Sasha Allen Announces He Signed with Republic Records

Sasha started his announcement video with a clip of him from January, sharing that he was about to call his school and drop out to focus on his music career. As he said, “I’m gonna be a successful musician, and let the universe do what it wants to do with me.”

He added that he would revisit the clip in a year to “see what I achieved.” Sasha then interrupted the clip to share that he looked “like a literal f*cking piece of feces in that video.” After dragging himself a bit more, he eventually dropped the news that he is “now signed to Republic Records, so it did f*cking work.”

Sasha kind of buried the lede there, but he elaborated in the comments to say that he actually signed two record deals, one as a duo with his dad, and another as a solo artist. As he wrote in the caption, “the last year has been wild.”

Jim and Sasha Made it to The Voice Semifinals

Sasha and his dad Jim were a duo on The Voice Season 21 last year. They competed on first-time coach Ariana Grande’s team, making it to the Semifinals before being eliminated.

The duo has been teasing new music for several months now, with Sasha posting photos from the recording studio in March. The singer has also posted song snippets and demos since his time on The Voice.

Sasha has earned a lot of fans since appearing on the show, as he currently has over 700,000 followers on TikTok. They shared their excitement for the singer’s news in the comments of his his new video. Fingers crossed we get to hear new music soon.
