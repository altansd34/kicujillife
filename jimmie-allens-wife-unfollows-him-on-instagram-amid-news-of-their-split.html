Country star Jimmie Allen and his wife Alexis Gale recently revealed that they had separated, at the same time they shared the news that they’re expecting a third child together. Gale has reportedly unfollowed Allen on Instagram.

Jimmie Allen’s Wife Alexis Gale Unfollows Him on Instagram

People reports that as of Tuesday, Gale was no longer following Allen on Instagram, although he was still following her. Gale also posted a cryptic message on her Instagram stories, writing, “The silence is loud enough for me.”

On Friday, the couple both posted to their individual accounts sharing the news that they had decided to separate. As they wrote, “Our number one priority is and always will be ensuring that our children are healthy, happy and loved, and we remain committed to co-parenting with love and respect for one another.”

The couple already has two daughters together — 3-year-old Naomi Bettie and 1-year-old Zara James. Allen also has an 8-year-old son named Aadyn from a previous relationship. Last year, Allen said in an interview with ET that he was “done” having kids. He later added that it was his wife’s idea to stop.

“But I’m cool if she’s done,” Allen said on the People Every Day podcast. “My jerseys are retired, but then we did talk about maybe in a couple years having another one, but we’ll kind of just see. Whatever happens, happens.”

The Couple Got Married in 2020

According to People, Allen and Gale first met through Allen’s cousin in 2019, when Gale was finishing up nursing school. The couple got married in 2020 in a private ceremony. The next year, they had a bigger celebration.

Allen told the outlet in 2021 that he never wanted to get married until he met Gale, saying, “I wanted to be a dad and do music, that’s it. I never wanted to get married. The whole husband thing was not something I ever wanted to do. But Alexis changed that. Now, I like it. It gives me some security — she can’t just be like, ‘I’m done.’ Divorce is a lot of paperwork!”

Since announcing the split last week, Allen has been promoting his new single “Be Alright.” On Wednesday, he posted a behind-the-scenes feature about the making of the song’s music video.
